Widespread Social and Economic Impact

Persistent, irrational fears and the disability to perform specific tasks in daily life such as driving and interacting with crowds plague about 8-9%, or over 25 million Americans. These anxiety disorders, such as phobias and PTSD, are just as widespread as diabetes in the U.S. Approximately two-thirds of individuals report not seeking treatment, and when left untreated, can experience self-feeding spirals of fear and avoidance behaviors. Phobias and PTSD were associated with estimated losses in productivity and personal earnings of about $200 billion in a 2008 US study, a figure which have likely increased in recent years.
